A full transcription of the Il Papa manuscript, with notes

Notes

  • Letters not directly represented, but interpolated from standard abbreviations, we have represented using parentheses. For instance, the word "ī" in the original text, we represent as "i(n)", and "ք" we represent as "p(er)".
  • [sic] in the text indicates clear and confirmed errors in the original (just so readers don't think the error is in our transcription), of course. Notes should, in all cases, explain why we have put the [sic] in.
  • And as should probably be obvious from context, the right-hand column contains notes about the transcription, where needed.
  • Any potential other translators using this transcription should note that the writer often sticks two words together with no intervening space, and we have left them rendered as such herein. For instance, on lines 7:14:3-4, he writs, "sischambino" instead of "si schambino". And the text is peppered with "conla" rather than "con la". We have not noted these specifically - there are simply to many, and we feel the clutter would detract from the comprehensibility of the whole.
