We have three thoughts for measures 60-65, none of which are wholly satisfactory:
  • "Straight towards one another" means around the circle. This is not satisfactory because he uses "in a circle" to mean that in other places.
  • "Straight towards one another" indicates facing, not direction of motion, and one does the P P t backwards, and the P P P forwards to end in the circle in which one began. This is unsatisfactory because it assumes a lot that isn't there.
  • Do what it litterally says for the P P t P P P - all backwards. Then, on the riprese, assume his "do these in a circle" means spiraling inward. This is unsatisfactory because it inserts a whole instruction that simply isn't there.
