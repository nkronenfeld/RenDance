la Primavera

By Giovannino

transcription and translation

meas. man 1 lady man 2 notes end position
1 R R R h. line
2 c c c c c c
3 s s s s s s
4 v v v
5 p p p p turning to face at the end triangle
6 r r r It just says they do "the riprese - number unspecified, but clearly plural. We here assume two full riprese, one left and one right.
7 r r r
8 c c c c c c
9 P P P forwards, presumably to almost meet - leave enough room to reverenza to one another near-line
10 P P P
11 R R R taking hands (but presumably still facing the opposite gender)
12 s s s s s s The directions here are very confusing, but amount to either going through one another to end in a triangle, or returning back the other way into a triangle. We strongly favor going through. triangle
13 v v v

Could be a no-time turn.

At this point, the text says, "and you must know that all that they do from this point on will be in the same tempo", which we would take to mean they are dancing in unison from here on - a statement he belies within 3 measures.
14 s s s s s s Presumably returning to their line near-line
15 v v v entirely unclear whether this is full or half. Perhaps the first pair of scempi brought one out only far enough so that the second could reverse the shallow triangle of the set, and these are half-turns, or perhaps the second scempi merely returned the set to a near-line (as we assume in the previous measure), and these are full turns?
16 s s s s

With right hands, switch places.

The text says she gives her right hand to the man on her right; we believe this is the first man, but due to the confusion of the previous few measures, there are certainly alternate interpretations.
17 p p p p p p p p switching to left hands, and returning places back
18 s s s s with the other man, this time left hands first (yes, left hands to two different men two measures in a row)
19 p p p p p p p p and right hands back
meas. man 1 lady man 2 notes end position
20 s s s s s s lady passing through, probably back the direction from which they last came triangle
21 r r r left - not turning yet
22 s s s s s s "and they turn themselves and do as much again on the right, and this through the center of the hall" - could be just the last ripresa but the "through the center of the hall" makes us think this includes the scempi too.
23 r r r
24 r r r "To the left"
25 r r r "To the right"
26 R R R
27 s s s s s s "coming together" shallower tirangle
28 v v v
28 R R R
29 s s s s s s near-line
30 v v v
31 t P t P

This draws a clear analogy to the previous place-switching, but the exact meaning is unclear. It could mean the hands switch as before, it could mean that the passo is instead 4 passetti (probably also with the hand-switching)

What seems incontroverible is:

  • It involves right hands first
  • They end up back in their own place
32 t P t P Same as last measure, but with left hands
33 t t t t t t A full hay, one switch per trapassino
34 t t t t t t
35 t t t t t t
36 s s s s s s backing away triangle
37 v v v
38 R R R
39 s s s s s s forwards near-line
40 v p p p p v lady returns to the middle while men turn line

I have an alternate interpretation where the trapassini take a full measure each, and the reverenza from measure 11 moves two measures later, and the one in measure 26 is dropped - this leaves the dances as a completely repeating structure, with a 10-measure verse, 5-measure chorus.
