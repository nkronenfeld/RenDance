Balletto called La Villanella, composed by Giovannino. First, they arrange themselves in a mezza luna. Then the lady gives her right hand to him who is on her left hand, and they make a reverenza to one another. Then the lady turns to him who is on her right hand, and giving one another right hands, they do the same reverenza as above. Then all three do in a circle two passi and a trapassino, and they do this same thing, in a circle, three times. Then they all do a doppio turning to the right hand. And then they do the riprese in a circle. And then they do a doppio turning to the left hand, and another to the right hand, all three of them, but in these [steps], not breaking the circle. Then follows the treccia of 12 passi in traverso between them all, in such a way that each of them comes to do four of them. And thus it begins: But first, you must know that when you give the right hand, you must move the foot opposite to it, that is, the left foot, wishing that you perform the passo in traverso. And giving the left hand, with this you must move the right foot. I say, then, that thus in the beginning, the lady first gives the right hand to him who is on the left hand, and he to her, and thus together they do a passo intraverso, and in the end they find themselves almost having changed places. Then he who remains in the middle gives his left hand. And thus they go, following, taking care not to fail in this rule: that he who is in the middle always gives the hand that is there, and thus they go, continuing in such a way that each one, as has been said, will have done 4 passi in traverso, and in the end, each one returns to his place. Then all of them do two scempi, and this is done in a circle, because when the said treccia has been done, they will find themselves again thus. And they continue and do 4 passetti and a doppio turning to the left hand, and one to the right hand. Then all together they do three trapassini and a passo. Then he who finds himself again in front, that is, at the left hand, passes in front of the lady, doing three more trapassini and a passo. And in this same time, he in the rear does three more, passing behind the lady, and thus they change places. And in this time, the lady does not move. Then the men stand still, and the lady does a doppio turning to the left hand, and another to the right hand. Then all together in a circle do three trapassini and a passo. Then all three together do two doppi turning, one to the left hand, and the other to the right hand. And it is... Finished.
