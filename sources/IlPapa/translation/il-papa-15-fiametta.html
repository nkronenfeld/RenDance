Cosmus Ticcius Faciebam Balletto called Fiammetta, composed by il Lanzino Four people dance - two men and two ladies - and each man has by the hand his lady on the right hand. And all four take hands, and thus holding hands, they arrange themselves in a file. And together they do everything that I say to do in this dance. And first they do a reverenza and the continenze, then two scempi. And those two who are in the middle do the volta, and in that same tempo, those two on the sides do 4 passetti, and they pass in front and turn to those who were in the middle, and they to them. Then they do two scempi opposite one another, and such that they come to meet one another. In this meeting, they must touch hands, and each to his lady, and they to them. And with 4 passetti, all pass one another, and turn in such a way that they come to be far by the 4 passetti that they have done. Then opposite one another they do two scempi and the volta. Then they do a reverenzetta forwards and one to the rear, and the volta di lasso. Then they do the riprese and a reverenzetta forwards and one backwards. And those who [illegible] find themselves in the middle do 4 passetti towards their companions, and in the same tempo, they do the volta, and where they were on the outside, they must return to the middle. Then all take hands, and together they do the mezze riprese and the volta. And thus they let go of hands, and then they do a reverenza. Then all together must do the treccia, and they begin thus: With those in the middle, who will be a lady and a man, turn thus to the sides, and they give their right hands to their companions, and they to them. And thus they do the said treccia, which is of 8 trapassini, and at the end of the said treccia, all must change places, that is, those from the inside found themselves outside, and those from outside return inside. Then all and in the same tempo do the volta. And it is... Finished.
